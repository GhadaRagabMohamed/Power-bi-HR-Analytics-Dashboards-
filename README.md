# HR Analytics Dashboards – Power BI Project

This repository contains **5 Power BI dashboards** analyzing employee attrition from multiple perspectives: Overview, Demographics, Work Factors, Compensation, and Recommendations.

## Project Goal
The main goal is to understand **why employees leave**, identify **high-risk groups**, and provide actionable **HR recommendations** to reduce turnover.

## Dashboards Overview

### 1. HR Overview
- Quick snapshot using **KPI cards**: total employees, attrition rate, average income, tenure.
- Insights: Younger employees (18–25), R&D department, and specific roles like Sales Executives have the highest attrition.
- Visuals: Bar+Line charts, Pie charts, Column charts.
- Recommendations: Mentoring programs, incentives, clear career paths.

### 2. Demographics Analysis
- Analyzes attrition by **age, gender, marital status, education**.
- Insights: Single employees, young staff (<25), and HR roles show higher attrition.
- Visuals: Stacked bars, Donut charts, Histograms.
- Recommendations: Targeted support for vulnerable groups, training programs, reduce overtime.

### 3. Work Factors Analysis
- Examines **overtime, business travel, work-life balance, department, role**.
- Insights: Frequent travelers, Sales and customer-facing roles, and overtime employees are high-risk.
- Visuals: KPIs, Clustered Bar, Treemap, Donut, Bar charts.
- Recommendations: Reduce overtime, flexible work options, workload redistribution, career mobility programs.

### 4. Compensation & Career Progression
- Studies the effect of **monthly income, role tenure, promotions, total experience** on attrition.
- Insights: Low-income, early-career, and employees with long promotion wait are most likely to leave.
- Visuals: KPI cards, Histograms, Horizontal Bar charts, Scatter plots.
- Recommendations: Competitive salary adjustments, accelerated promotions, structured career paths.

### 5. Insights & Recommendations
- Attrition driven by **job demands, compensation, career progression**, and demographics.
- Recommended actions: Workload balancing, career growth, salary review, mentoring, flexible policies.

## Tools & Data
- **Power BI Desktop** for dashboard creation
- **Dataset**: WA_Fn-UseC_-HR-Employee-Attrition.csv (1,470 employees)

## Conclusion
This project provides **data-driven insights** for HR teams to retain talent, enhance employee satisfaction, and reduce turnover by targeting high-risk groups and work conditions.
